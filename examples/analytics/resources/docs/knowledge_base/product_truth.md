# Product Truth - Business Logic & Institutional Knowledge

This document captures business logic, institutional knowledge, and product insights learned during SQL development work. Update this document when you learn important context that will help future SQL work.

## What EvenUp Does

EvenUp is a SaaS platform for personal injury law firms that uses AI to automate case work and maximize settlement outcomes. Our customers are attorneys who represent injured plaintiffs and negotiate with insurance carriers on contingency.

### Platform Overview

EvenUp hosts a SaaS platform for personal injury case management and demand generation.

#### Portal (Current Platform)
The primary client-facing application where law firm users manage cases and request demands.

**Core concepts:**
- **Matter**: Usually one case, can represent multiple plaintiffs (dim_matters)
- **Doc request (request, questionnaire)**: A demand letter/package that EvenUp drafts to send to insurance companies to support settlement claims
  - **Expert doc**: EvenUp employees (LOPS) use AI assistant to write; used for complicated or high-value cases
  - **AI draft**: Any self-serve document request generated by the user — spans multiple `request_type` values: Express Demand (XD), Mirror Mode (MM), Complaints, Negotiation Briefs, Medical Summaries, Blank Drafts, and others. ⚠️ AI Drafts is NOT synonymous with "Mirror Mode + Express Demand" — those are two specific `request_type` values that fall under the AI Drafts umbrella, but AI Drafts is the wider category.
- **Files (document, page)**: Supporting documents related to the case - medical records, medical bills, police reports, insurance documents, etc.

#### System of Work (SOW)
A comprehensive CRM/case management system for law firms, intended to eventually supersede Portal. Includes case intake, matter lifecycle, client management, billing, and other practice management features.

**Current state:** SOW is **live in production** as a separate React app (`lops-frontend/apps/sow/`), distinct from Portal (`lops-frontend/webapp/`). Both ship from the same monorepo and emit into the same Amplitude project. Pageviews on `/sow/%` identify SOW traffic (~0.3% of total pageview volume as of 2026-04-21). **Dbt coverage for SOW-specific entities is still being built out** — expect gaps in the data model for matters/leads/contacts that don't exist on the Portal side. See `frontend_events.md` for SOW-vs-LOPS event attribution rules.

#### Overall Workflow

**Pre-Demand (Case Preparation):**
1. **Case creation**: Client creates matter (manual entry via Portal or automated via CI)
2. **File upload**: Supporting documents uploaded (manual or via CI)
3. **AI annotation**: Files are processed by AI pipelines (FLP/CLP) to extract structured data:
   - Page classification (medical record, medical bill, police report, etc.)
   - Medical bill extraction (charges, procedure codes, dates)
   - Provider identification and extraction
   - Date of service extraction
   - Key case facts (injury details, liability information)
4. **Missing document check**: Validation that each medical bill has corresponding records and vice versa
5. **Medical chronology generation**: AI produces a timeline document tracing the plaintiff's treatment journey from injury through recovery
6. **Demand drafting**: AI generates demand letter/package with medical summary, liability analysis, and settlement valuation

**Post-Demand (Settlement Support):**
After demand delivery, clients continue using Portal for:
- **Negotiation support**: W&I products (AI Playbooks, Case Companion) help identify strengths/weaknesses during settlement discussions
- **Litigation preparation**: Tools analyze case facts and treatment gaps if case proceeds to trial

**Core Product Categories:**

**1. Demand Generation** - Automated demand letter/package creation
   - **Standard/Basic+ Demands**: Traditional demand drafting with 1-3 day turnaround, minimal customer editing needed
   - **Express Demands (XD)**: Fast-turnaround demands for "easy-tender" cases (MVA, Premises Liability, Dog Bite), requires customer editing in Workstation
   - Value: 30-100% higher settlements vs. manual demands, 3+ months faster resolution, reduced case manager overhead

**2. Case Intelligence Tools (W&I - Workflows & Insights)**
   - **AI Playbooks**: Custom question sets that extract information from case files automatically (on-demand or autorun)
   - **Bills Summary**: AI-extracted medical bill totals and provider breakdowns with smart deduplication
   - **Case Companion**, **Missing Document Check**, and other workflow automation tools

**3. Document Editing & Customization**
   - **Workstation**: In-browser demand editor in lops-frontend. Umbrella term covering two editor paths — **Standard / Expert Demand** (LOPS-drafted, at `/demands/%/form/%`) and **Express Demand (XD)** (customer-driven, at `/cases/%/express-demand/%`) — plus **Mirror Mode (MM)** as a template mode within it. See `workstation.md` for structure + events.
   - **AI Edits**: Section-level AI-powered revisions (tone, length, content adjustments)
   - **Mirror Mode (MM)**: Template-based drafting where the user uploads a sample document; AI mirrors its format, tone, and structure to generate new documents in that style. **Not limited to demands** — firms also use MM for Complaints, Medical Summaries, Initial Disclosures, and other custom documents. Uses Workstation with template-mirroring generation logic; accessed via `/cases/%/express-demand/templates/%`. Amplitude prefix: `[FE][MM]`. Distinct from SOW Templates at `/sow/settings/templates/%` (separate system in the SOW app). See `workstation.md`.
     - **Data marker**: `fact_self_serve_request.is_custom_template = TRUE` identifies MM requests.
     - **Identifying MM document type**: Join `fact_self_serve_request.custom_template_id → stg_lops_sql__public_selfserve_customtemplate.id` and read the `document_type` column. ⚠️ `document_type` is free-form text entered by users when they upload the template — EvenUp does not enforce a canonical enum. Values seen in the wild: `"Holt MVA Demand"`, `"Liability Demand"`, `"UM Demand"`, `"Initial Disclosure"`, `"Medical Summary"`. Filter with `LIKE` or a known list rather than an exact match.

## CMS Integrations & Syncing Strategy

**How It Works:**
- EvenUp integrates with clients' Case Management Systems (CMS) to import cases
- Import is criteria-based: clients specify which case types, statuses, etc. to sync (e.g., "only sync Treatment and Litigation")
- "External case status" = status in client's CMS (varies by system, not standardized)
- EvenUp internal status = only OPEN or CLOSED

**Selective Syncing Model:**
- Cases sync only while within configured criteria
- Syncing stops when: (1) case moves outside configured statuses, OR (2) demand is requested
- Post-demand syncing available via feature flag (~10 firms only, cost being assessed)
- Currently syncs files post-demand, but NOT case-level data

**Product Evolution - Critical Context:**
- **Original design**: Integrations built for point-in-time demand generation
- **Current reality**: W&I products (AI Playbooks, Bills Summary, MDC, Case Companion) need ongoing sync throughout case lifecycle
- **Tension**: Most cases stop syncing after demand request, limiting W&I effectiveness
- **No historical tracking**: Only current status at last sync stored, no timestamps for status changes

**Implications for Data:**
- External case status unreliable for closed cases (stopped syncing before closure)
- Best for queries on active/recently synced cases only
- If case has recent file uploads → likely still syncing → status should be current

## General Terminology

- **Matter:** A legal case (synonymous with "Case" in data models)
- **Portal:** The current, and soon to be legacy, app that our clients interface with
- **matter_id:** Unique identifier for a matter; appears in public URLs as `https://evenup.law/cases/{matter_id}`
- **LOPs:** Legal Operations team (human experts who create demands and verify AI output)
- **Ground Truth (GT):** Human-verified data used for evaluation (typically from LOPs)
- **FLP:** File level pipeline. An AI processing pipeline that extracts information from legal and medical documents
- **CLP:** AI processing pipeline that uses results from FLP to connect the dots
- **CI (Continuous Integration):** Automated case processing pipeline
- **W&I:** Workflows & Insights product bundle (includes Express Demands, Case Companion, MDC, AI Playbooks, etc.)
- **_et suffix:** Eastern Time timestamps (e.g., `first_completed_at_et`)
- **Case strength & weakness:** CSW — also known as value driver & detractor (VDD) in engineering / data, and as **Case Flags** in the UI. A W&I product. Amplitude events use the `[Value Drivers Detractors]` prefix.
- **Kili Platform:** Third-party annotation management platform used to coordinate human labelers for ground truth annotation work. Models containing "kili" in their name (e.g., `dim_groundtruth_kili_assets`, `stg_analytics_prod__kili_events`, `fact_annotation_kili_assets`) are related to the annotation process and human labeling workflows.

## Document Classification - Common Confusion

**Don't confuse these two concepts:**

**Document Request Type** (`dim_casejoin_document_requests.document_type`)
- What product/document the user requested: Standard, Basic+, Express, MedChron, etc.
- Two sources: `intake_questionnaire` (traditional demands) and `self_serve` (W&I products)
- `page_count` field = total case pages at request time (NOT pages for that specific document)

**Page Classification** (`stg_annotation__page_v2.page_classification`)
- What type of document FLP detected: MEDICAL_RECORD, MEDICAL_BILL, POLICE_REPORT, INSURANCE_BILL, etc.
- Classification happens at page level during FLP processing
- OTHER = non-medical documents (correspondence, legal docs)

### File Upload ID

**What it is:** UUID from File Upload Service identifying the uploaded file.

**Key relationship:** One `file_upload_id` can have multiple annotation files (re-annotations).

**Querying current file state:**
- When querying "current state of file" (latest classification, latest entities, etc.):
  - Use only the most recent annotation for each `file_upload_id`
  - Exclude rows where `file_upload_id` IS NULL (older data or non-FUS ingestion paths)
